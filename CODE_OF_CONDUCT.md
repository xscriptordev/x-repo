<h1 align="center"> Code of Conduct </h1>

<div align="center">

<h2 align="center" id="our-standards"> Our Standards </h2>
<ul>
  <li>Use respectful and inclusive language in all community interactions.</li>
  <li>Accept constructive feedback gracefully to improve our themes and resources.</li>
  <li>Focus on what is best for the community and the Xscriptor project.</li>
  <li>Show empathy toward other contributors and users of JetBrains IDEs.</li>
</ul>

<h2 align="center" id="unacceptable-behavior"> Unacceptable Behavior </h2>
<ul>
  <li>Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind.</li>
  <li>Personal attacks, insults, or trolling in issues, PRs, or discussions.</li>
  <li>Publishing private information without explicit permission.</li>
  <li>Any behavior that creates an unsafe or hostile environment.</li>
</ul>

<h2 align="center" id="scope"> Scope </h2>
<p align="center">
  This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issue trackers, pull requests, and any other community channels related to this repository.
</p>

<h2 align="center" id="reporting"> Reporting </h2>
<p align="center">
  If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the maintainers at:
  <br />
  <a href="mailto:x@xscriptor.com"><b>x@xscriptor.com</b></a>
</p>

<h2 align="center" id="enforcement"> Enforcement </h2>
<p>
  Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.
</p>

<hr />

<div align="center">
  <p>By participating in this project, you agree to follow these standards and help us maintain a professional environment for all developers.</p>
</div>
